Political Science

About

Political Science is one of the fundamental Arts stream subjects which deals with the state and systems of government and scientific analysis of various political activities and their behaviour. Administrative theories and concepts of the domain cover numerous subfields including Political Economy, International Relations, Comparative Politics, Public Policy, and Political Methodology. BA Political Science followed by an MA in Political Science can help you build a strong career in this specialized field of Social Science. Further, the Political Science syllabus helps individuals discover a wide spectrum of a potential career in Political Science such as political office holders, law firms, law enforcement agencies, political organization, field research firms and many more.

Political Science Syllabus 2022

The 12th political Science is the best arts subjects in 12th class covers the world politics and Indian Politics since independence

1. Contemporary World Politics

” Cold war era” –

  • Topics of this unit – Emergence of two power blocs after the second world war
  • Challenges of bipolarity
  • India and the cold war.

“The end of bipolarity” –

It is all about

  • New entities in world politics – Russia
  • Balkan State and central Asian states
  • Introduction of democratic politics
  • India’s relationship with Russia and other post-communist countries.

“US Hegemony in world politics” – 

It is all about the growth of unilateralism – Afghanistan, the first gulf war, response to 9/11 and attack on Iraq, India’s renegotiation of its with the USA.

“Alternative centers of power” – 

It covers topics like the Rising of China as an economic power in post-maoera, creation and expansion of the European Union, ASEAN.

India’s relations with China.

“Contemporary South Asia in the post-cold war era” – 

It is all about Democratisation in Pakistan and Nepal. Ethnic conflict in Sri Lanka, India’s relations with its neighbours.

“International organizations” – 

It is all about restructuring and the future of the UN. India’s position in the restructured UN. India’s relations with its neighbours.

“Security in the contemporary world” – 

It includes traditional concerns of security and politics of disarmament. Human security- global poverty, health and education.

Issues of human rights and migration.

“Environment and the natural resources” – 

It is all about environment movement and the evolution of global environmental norms.

Conflicts over traditional and common poverty resources.

India’s stand-in global environmental debates.

“Globalization” –

This unit covers topics like Economic, cultural and political manifestations.

Debates on the nature of the consequences of globalization.

India as an arena of globalization and struggle against it.

2. Politics in India since independence

“Challenges of nation-building” – 

This unit includes information about Nehru’s approach to nation-building – the legacy of partition, challenges of refugees, resettlement, the Kashmir problem.

“The era of one-party dominance” – 

It is all about the general elections at the national level and state level, opposition parties.

“Politics of planned development” – 

It includes information about five-year plans, expansion of the state sector and the rise of new economic interest. Green revolution and its political fallouts.
