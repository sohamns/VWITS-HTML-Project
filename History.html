History

About

One of the traditional Arts stream subjects, History teaches the evolution of human civilization from prehistory to the present times. It also covers the major historical events, lays emphasis on the art and architecture such as the history of Indian Art, and imparts knowledge on the works of great personalities of the world. The curriculum of the subject takes you on a journey of mankind and develops your insight into the future of development. On pursuing courses like BA History, MA History, etc, you will gain knowledge pertaining to Ancient History, Medieval History, Modern History, Political History, Cultures of the World, Language and Literature, amongst others. Graduates in History can enter into professions like Teaching, Research, Conservation Management, Archaeology, Civil Services, Public Administration, Travel and Tourism and Media Studies. Also, you will easily find History in the syllabus of BA courses after 12th Arts.

History Syllabus 2022

The book that studies different eras of Indian history ranging from Harappan civilization to colonialism in India is divided into three parts and each part has different themes. It covers each topic in detail giving you a complete overview of the various historical periods and the events leading to them.

Part I

Part one of the book has four chapters that elucidate the earlier phase of the history that is marked by the Harappan Civilization followed by the chapters that study the social structure, politics, architecture, and philosophy of that era. Given below are the contents in the part-I of class 12 History book:

Unit No. Title Unit
1 Bricks, Beads, and Bones The Harappan Civilisation
2 Kings, Farmers, and Towns Early States and Economies(c.600 BCE-600 CE)
3 Kinship, Caste, and Class Early Societies(c. 600 BCE-600 CE)
4 Thinkers, Beliefs, and Buildings Cultural Developments(c. 600 BCE-600 CE)

Part II

The second part of the class 12 History book consists of five chapters that study the traditions, accounts by travellers, religious philosophies, kingdoms and social class prevalent in ancient India. Here are the contents in the part-II of class 12 History book:

Unit No. Title Unit
5 Through the Eyes of Travellers Perceptions of Society(c. tenth to seventeenth centuries)
6 Bhakti –Sufi Traditions: Changes in Religious Beliefs and Devotional Texts(c. eighth to eighteenth centuries)
7 An Imperial Capital: Vijayanagara (c. fourteenth to sixteenth centuries)
8 Peasants, Zamindars and the State Agrarian Society and the Mughal Empire(c. sixteenth-seventeenth centuries)
9 Kings and Chronicles The Mughal Courts(c. sixteenth-seventeenth centuries)

Part III

The last part of the book has six chapters that study the recent historical developments in India. From East India Company’s subjugation and freedom struggle to framing the constitution after independence, the book gives a comprehensive understanding of the issues. Given below are the contents in the part-III of class 12 History book:

Unit No. Title Unit
10 Colonialism and the Countryside Exploring Official Archives
11 Rebels and the Raj 1857 Revolt and its Representations
12 Colonial Cities Urbanization, Planning, and Architecture
13 Mahatma Gandhi and the Nationalist Movement Civil Disobedience and Beyond
14 Understanding Partition Politics, Memories, Experiences
15 Framing the Constitution The Beginning of a New Era
